Our Point of View

You've scaled the footprint. Now scale the relationship.

Sun Auto's acquisition-led growth model is working. The "National Reach, Neighborhood Feel" positioning is genuinely differentiated — you've kept the local identities intact while building real operational scale. That's hard to do, and it creates a moat.

But every acquisition brings another customer database. Another service history file. Another email list that doesn't talk to the others. At 575 locations and 50+ brands, the same driver is a different person in every system they've ever walked into.

The next chapter of Sun Auto's growth isn't won at the acquisition table — it's won in the follow-up text that lands exactly when an oil change is overdue, the win-back campaign that catches a lapsed customer before they switch, and the fleet account that gets called before renewal is ever at risk.

That requires a unified customer record, an autonomous marketing engine, and AI agents working the accounts you don't have the headcount to work manually. That's exactly what Agentforce, Marketing Cloud, and Data Cloud do.

Fragmented identity across 50+ brands

The same driver appears as a different customer in every acquired system. You can't build loyalty with someone you don't recognize — regardless of how many times they've serviced with you.

No early signal on retention risk

Without cross-brand behavioral data, churn is invisible until it's already happened. The customer who stopped coming in 60 days ago looks no different from one who's coming back next week.

Marketing that doesn't scale with acquisitions

Every new brand adds another disconnected marketing operation. The headcount required to run personalized communications across this footprint grows linearly — but the revenue opportunity doesn't have to.

Fleet opportunity managed reactively

Fleet accounts need proactive, relationship-driven engagement. Without a unified account view, fleet is managed by whoever picks up the phone — and fleet customers who feel neglected don't stay.
